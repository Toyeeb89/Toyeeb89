Course Summary

Artificial Intelligence (AI) now impacts nearly every area of our personal and professional lives. The application of AI, particularly computer vision, has tremendous implications for clinical dentistry. We now know that incipient decay can be diagnosed up to 5 years before the naked eye will pick up the lesion intraorally or radiographically and that the proliferation of periodontal disease can impact overall health. The use-case for AI-augmented radiographs is clear. In this course, clinicians will be introduced to the umbrella terms used to describe components of AI. Participants will understand applications for AI use in general health care and dentistry. AI has the potential to change the nature of dental practice many areas from patient management to treatment plan acceptance and reimbursement. As such, this primer will preview the why and how of implementing AI into your practice.

Course Summary

•

Define Artificial Intelligence and other terms related to the field.

•

Understand applications of AI in medicine/health care.

•

Discover examples of the use of AI in dentistry.

•

Learn about the future of AI in clinical dentistry.
